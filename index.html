Things I've Said

I like writing (and talking) about Product Management, team building and mental health.

In 2020 I set myself the goal of improving my public speaking skills. I was honoured to be accepted to give a talk at JAM London where I spoke about how product teams at Farewill bring Delight to the Death Industry. After lots of practice, and help from a speaking coach, I feel really proud of my talk. I've had lots of positive feedback afterwards too.

Also in 2020, myself and Victor Hwang were invited on the 11fs Pulse podcast to talk about how product teams at Farewill approach understanding our customers in service of modernising the death industry, how we empower our internal teams with great tools, and we walk through the thinking behind some of our user journeys.

I've written a really detailed explainer on how my team and I set up, run, and analyse customer interviews in 7 days. I noticed the repeating tension between the desire to do customer discovery vs the perceived time it takes so I hoped this'd help debunk some of that. I've heard that the article is used as a training resource for other startups in the Kindred portfolio, and at EF.

I've also written and spoken about mental health, stress, and burnout quite openly after struggling with those things myself. This post about de-stressing by going for a walk just to have a walk gets regular positive feedback and has sparked a few quite moving conversations with people I'd otherwise have never known.
